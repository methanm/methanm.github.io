Sermon on the Mount in James

I think we might note that there is a lot more than just references back to Matthew.  In some cases, it also seems to line up with Luke, who includes among the beatitudes a set of woes also. Luke's list is also smaller:  he has the poor, hungry, weeping, persecuted.  James seems to use them often.

The references to adultery also seem to fit with the various sermons.  So also do references to the flower of the field, although the context is somewhat different.

There are a lot of interconnections in James as well:  the business about being rich appears in many places, as does the the issue of double-mindedness, et c.

It's worth noting that Luke also lists corresponding woes that are more in line with James' text than Matthew's might be.  Matthew provides different woes in ch 23.

It's also worth noting that Luke's list seems less "spiritual" than Matthews.  He says, "Blessed are you poor" rather than Matthew's "blessed are the poor in spirit."  Also "blessed are you hungry" rather than "blessed are those who hunger and thirst after righteousness."
